Newton's First Law - The Law of Inertia

Newton's First Law states that an object at rest will remain at rest, and an object in motion will stay in motion with the same speed and in the same direction unless acted upon by an unbalanced force.

Newton's Second Law - F = ma

Newton's Second Law states that the acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass. It is mathematically represented as F = ma.

Newton's Third Law - Action and Reaction

Newton's Third Law states that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. In other words, if object A exerts a force on object B, object B will exert an equal and opposite force on object A.

Conclusion

Understanding Newton's laws of motion is essential for comprehending the behavior of objects in the physical world. These laws have far-reaching implications in various fields of science and engineering.
